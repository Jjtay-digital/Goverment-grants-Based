Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG) – AI

Focus area: Standard AI Solutions

Expanded in 2026 to cover a wider range of pre-approved, off-the-shelf AI tools (e.g. customer service bots, automated accounting).

For local SMEs looking for IT solutions and equipment to improve business productivity.

Visit the Business Grants Portal for application status. Successful applications are subject to grant conditions. Incomplete applications will be rejected and may be resubmitted with complete documents. EDGE launches in 2H2026. Existing grants (EDG, MRA, PSG) remain accessible until launch.

Apply

About this programme

The Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG) helps Singapore companies improve their productivity and automate existing processes through IT solutions and equipment.

  • Up to 50% of eligible costs for local SMEs — Receive up to $30,000 and improve your business productivity.
  • Supports sector-specific and generic solutions — Get support for sector-specific and generic solutions that are pre-approved by EnterpriseSG and other participating agencies. You may find the full list of solutions on GoBusiness Gov Assist.

Retrospective applications (grant applicant must not have made payment, and/or any form of deposits prior to application submission) will not be supported. Group applications (two or more companies listed as applicants in one application form) are not allowed. Each UEN should be associated with only one application submission.

Eligibility

To be eligible for the PSG grant, the company must meet these criteria:

  • Business entity is registered and operating in Singapore
  • Company has at least 30% local equity held directly or indirectly by Singaporean(s) and/or Singapore PR(s), determined by the ultimate individual ownership
  • Group annual sales turnover not exceeding S$100 million OR group employment size not exceeding 200 employees
  • IT solutions or equipment that are purchased, leased, or subscribed to must be used in Singapore
  • Grant applicant must not have made payment, and/or any form of deposits to a supplier, vendor or third party in relation to the purchase/lease/subscription prior to application submission
  • Company does not fall under: Charities, Institutions of Public Characters (IPCs), Religious Entities, Voluntary Welfare Organisation (VWO), Government agencies and subsidiaries, and societies

Employers eligible for the SkillsFuture Enterprise Credit can qualify for additional subsidies under the scheme.

How to apply

  1. Identify relevant solutions — Visit GoBusiness Gov Assist to access a full list of adoptable solutions. Identify the relevant solutions for your needs, together with a suitable vendor.
  2. Get direct quotations — IT solutions: get a quotation from pre-approved vendor (items and cost as per Annex 3 of the selected PSG solution package). Equipment: source and get a quotation. Consultancy: get a quotation from pre-approved consultant. Only the actual purchase of the pre-approved solution is supported (excludes delivery, installation). No compulsory application fees from EnterpriseSG.
  3. Prepare your documents — Contact details (main contact, Letter of Offer addressee); Company’s Financial Statements for the past 3 years; Proposal and/or quotation from vendor (detailed scope, cost breakdown, etc.); Deployment location / Tenancy agreement if deploying to non-ACRA address; Business impact description.
  4. Apply on the Business Grant Portal (BGP) — Sign in with Corppass. Each complete application usually takes 6 weeks to process. Third-party applications or management are not permitted.
  5. Receive your Letter of Offer — If awarded, accept within the specified timeline. Details include conditions of support, amount awarded, project qualifying period, name of equipment/IT solution.
  6. Complete your project — Ensure solution/equipment is purchased or subscribed as approved, deployed and installed for at least 30 days, and paid in full.
  7. Submit your claims — Via BGP by the claim due date: Invoice (addressed to applicant, from approved vendor); Payment documents (proof of full payment); Usage report for IT solutions; Pictures (license number for IT solutions, serial number for equipment).
  8. Receive your disbursements — Approved claim amount disbursed via Corporate PayNow or GIRO. Disbursement takes approximately 14 working days (PayNow) following approval. Set up PayNow Corporate or GIRO; without it, disbursement cannot be made.

Enterprise Compute Initiative (ECI)

Focus area: Infrastructure

A S$150 million fund providing access to high-end cloud compute resources and engineering support for AI development. Singapore-based enterprises can receive up to S$105,000 grant funding through the ECI AI Accelerator, supported by Microsoft and Digital Industry Singapore (DISG).

Apply

Funding & scope

  • Funding: Up to S$105,000 through the Singapore Government’s Enterprise Compute Initiative (ECI).
  • Scope: Supports Singapore-based enterprises in adopting AI to drive business growth and operational transformation.
  • Approval: Fast approval within 5 working days. You will need Corppass login, audited financials, and a clearly defined AI use case.

How to qualify for ECI funding

Your organisation should meet the following baseline requirements:

  • Singapore-registered entity — Registered or incorporated and physically operating in Singapore.
  • Executive sponsorship — A CEO or equivalent-level leader must sponsor and support the initiative.
  • Minimum local workforce — At least 10 employees based in Singapore.
  • Established tech capabilities — A technology team of at least 2 members (e.g. software engineers, AI engineers, and/or data scientists).
  • AI development experience — Prior experience building a custom AI solution for proof of concept and/or wider deployment.
  • Data readiness — Accessible datasets available to support the proposed AI use case.
  • Financial capacity — Adequate financial resources to ensure successful project completion.
  • Use case assessment — Proposed AI use case must deliver business or industry impact through process improvement, new products, R&D, or ecosystem contribution.

Where AI can create impact

Front office: Deliver 24/7 support with AI-powered digital assistants; reduce response times and improve accuracy; empower teams to focus on high-value, strategic tasks.

Back office: Automate document management (classification, retrieval, workflow approvals); simplify processes across Finance, HR, Operations, and Sales; minimise errors, save time, and reduce compliance risks.

The ECI grant is flexible and designed to support your AI ambitions. Register your interest or apply via the link above.

Startup SG Equity

Focus area: Early-stage and growth-stage tech startups

A co-investment scheme by Enterprise Singapore and the Singapore Economic Development Board to facilitate early-stage funding for Singapore-based technology startups. Budget 2026 added S$1 billion to support deep tech startups at both early and growth stages.

Apply

Eligibility

To qualify for Startup SG Equity, startups must demonstrate:

  • Committed and capable management team with relevant experience and business acumen
  • Clear value proposition and potential for scalability in target markets, including international expansion
  • Commercially viable business model with evidence of pilot or paying customers
  • Substantial innovative and/or intellectual property content developed or owned in-house

Sector focus

The programme focuses on Health and Biomedical Sciences, Urban Solutions and Sustainability, and Advanced Manufacturing and Engineering. Startups from other sectors may be considered if they meet the key business attributes.

Investment parameters

  • General tech: Up to S$2 million investment cap (70% government co-investment up to S$250,000, then 1:1 up to S$2 million)
  • Deep tech: Up to S$4 million investment cap (70% government co-investment up to S$500,000, then 1:1 up to S$4 million)

SPRING SEEDS Capital Pte Ltd administers the scheme and co-invests alongside private investors. Apply through appointed investment partners or directly to SEEDS Capital via the link above.

Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS)

Focus area: Tax Incentives

The EIS is available for YA 2024 to YA 2028. Below is a summary of how the EIS benefits you (source: IRAS).

Apply

How the EIS Benefits You

Tax deductions / allowances Cash payout
400% tax deductions/allowances on up to $400,000 of qualifying expenditure per year for each of the 4 qualifying activities: Qualifying R&D undertaken in Singapore; Registration of IPs; Acquisition and licensing of IPRs; Training courses eligible for SSG funding and aligned with the Skills Framework. Option to convert1 up to $100,000 of the total qualifying expenditure across all qualifying activities for each YA into a non-taxable cash payout at a conversion rate of 20%, in lieu of tax deductions and/or allowances.
400% tax deductions on up to $50,000 of expenditure per year for: Innovation projects carried out with polytechnics, the ITE or other qualified partners.2 Cash payout option is not available for qualifying AI expenditures.
[NEW!] 400% tax deductions/allowances on up to $50,000 of expenditure per year for: Qualifying AI expenditures.3

1 Specific conditions apply to the conversion of qualifying expenditure incurred under “registration of IPs” and “acquisition and licensing of IPRs”.
2 As announced in Budget 2026, the list of qualified partners will be expanded to include the Sectoral AI Centre of Excellence for Manufacturing for YA 2027 and YA 2028.
3 As announced in Budget 2026, the list of qualifying activities will be expanded to include qualifying AI expenditures for YA 2027 and YA 2028. IRAS will release more details by mid-2026.
